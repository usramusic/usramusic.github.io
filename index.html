A DIFFUSION MODEL WITH SELF-REPRESENTATION ALIGNMENT FOR

VIDEO-TO-MUSIC GENERATION

Overview

This is the demo page of our work: A Diffusion Model with Self-Representation Alignment for Video-to-Music Generation. Detailed demonstrations will be provided soon.

We propose a novel diffusion U-Net architecture enhanced by explicitly aligning the weaker representation from the earlier layer conditioned on higher noise with the better representation from the later layer conditioned on lower noise during training, which we use for the challenging multimodal task of video background music generation. Our diffusion U-Net Self-Representation Alignment (U-SRA) method employs an alignment loss to effectively guide our diffusion model to learn good internal representations, thereby enabling it to produce higher-quality symbolic music conditioned on video features. We employ a cross-attention mechanism to synchronize music with video segments.
